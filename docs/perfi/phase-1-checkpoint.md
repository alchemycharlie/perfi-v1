## Key decisions captured

- Manual-first is a product principle, not a temporary limitation
- Benefits income is a first-class income type, not an afterthought
- Neurodiversity-conscious UX is the default experience, not a mode
- GBP only in v1 — no multi-currency
- No Open Banking, bank sync, or third-party APIs in v1
- Admin panel ships at launch, scoped to user/waitlist/subscription management
- Free + £4.99/month paid tier from day one
- Web-first, responsive — no native mobile apps in v1

## Contradictions and risks identified

- **"As fully featured as practical" admin panel** is vague — needs concrete scoping in Phase 2
- **Manual entry friction** is the single biggest churn risk — UX for quick entry must be excellent
- **Accessibility claims** must be backed by actual WCAG compliance, not just intent
- **Dark mode** is deferred but should be designed for in the system from the start to avoid retrofit cost
- **Notifications** are out of scope but pay-date and bill-date reminders are high-value — revisit for v1.1
